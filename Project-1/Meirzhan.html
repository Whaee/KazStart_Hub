Marcus Licinius Crassus

Marcus Licinius Crassus - ancient Roman commander and politician, consul of 70 and 55 BC, participant of the first triumvirate, one of the richest people of his time. He belonged to the noble plebeian family of Liciniev. In his youth, he began his career as a court speaker, but was forced to interrupt it because of civil wars.
